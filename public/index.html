Where to Find Your Fan's ID Plate

The ID plate is a small metal or plastic label showing the manufacturer, model number, and specifications.

Wall-Mounted Fans

Behind the front grille or on the back of the unit. You may need to remove the cover to see it.

Inline / Duct Fans

On the motor casing, visible from the side of the unit. Usually accessible without removing from the duct.

Centrifugal Fans

On the scroll housing or motor mount. Look around the back and sides of the unit.

MVHR Units

On the side panel of the unit, usually accessible from the loft or plant room without removing.

Plate worn or missing? Send us a photo of the fan itself — the duct size, housing shape, anything you can see. We've been doing this for 40+ years. We can usually work it out.

What Information Is On a Fan ID Plate?

Manufacturer & Model

The most important bit — tells us exactly what fan you have.

Voltage & Frequency

230V/50Hz (single-phase) or 400V/50Hz (three-phase).

Airflow (m³/h or l/s)

The duty — essential for matching a replacement.

Power (W) & Current (A)

Motor size — helps us find the right equivalent.

Part / SKU Number

Sometimes different from the model name.

Speed (RPM)

Useful for matching performance.

IP Rating

IP44 for wet areas, IP54 for dusty environments.

Date of Manufacture

Helps us know if the model is current or discontinued.

Common Questions

The fan model is discontinued — can you still find a replacement?

Usually, yes. Most manufacturers publish cross-reference guides for discontinued models. We carry current equivalents from Vent-Axia, Systemair, S&P, Elta, and others. If the original model is gone, we'll find you something that matches the duty, duct size, and mounting.

I need to replace a commercial fan but the ductwork can't change. Can you match the connection?

That's the most common requirement. Send us the duct diameter and the airflow duty — we'll spec a fan that fits the existing installation.

How do I know what size extractor fan I need?

It depends on the room volume and the required air change rate. For bathrooms, Building Regs require 15 l/s (54 m³/h). For commercial kitchens, you might need 20-30 air changes per hour. Send us the room dimensions and use — we'll calculate it for you.

Can you supply the same brand, or do I need to switch?

We stock all the major brands — Vent-Axia, Systemair, S&P, Elta, Greenwood, Nuaire, Xpelair, and more. If the original brand is available, we'll quote it. If not, we'll recommend the closest equivalent.

What if the ID plate is missing or unreadable?

Send us whatever you got — a photo of the fan itself, the duct size, where it's installed. Our team has 40+ years of experience identifying fans. We can usually work it out from the housing shape, motor type, and application.

I need to replace 20+ fans across a building. Can you help?

Absolutely — that's our bread and butter. Send us the details and we'll put together a full schedule with quantities, lead times, and pricing.

How quickly can you get the replacement to me?

If we stock the replacement or equivalent, it's next-day delivery. For non-stock items or specialist orders, we'll give you an honest lead time.
